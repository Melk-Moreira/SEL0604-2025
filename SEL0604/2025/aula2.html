Revisão de Álgebra Linear

Representação de um vetor na base $A=\{\hat a_1,\cdots,\hat a_n\}$

$$ \vec v = \alpha_1\hat{a}_1+\alpha_2\hat a_2+\cdots +\alpha_n \hat a_n $$
$$ v^A = \begin{bmatrix} \alpha_1\\ \alpha_2 \\ \vdots \\ \alpha_n \end{bmatrix} $$

Revisão de Álgebra Linear

Representação de um vetor na base $A=\{\hat a_1,\cdots,\hat a_n\}$

$$ \langle \hat a_i, \vec v\rangle = \langle \hat a_i, (\alpha_1\hat{a}_1+\alpha_2\hat a_2+\cdots +\alpha_n \hat a_n)\rangle $$
$$ = \alpha_1\langle \hat a_i, \hat{a}_1\rangle +\alpha_2\langle \hat a_i,\hat a_2\rangle +\cdots +\alpha_n \langle \hat a_i,\hat a_n\rangle $$
$$ = \alpha_i\langle \hat a_i, \hat{a}_i\rangle $$
$$ \Rightarrow\alpha_i = \langle \hat a_i, \vec v\rangle $$

Revisão de Álgebra Linear

As coordenadas são as projeções nos vetores da base!

$$ \Rightarrow\alpha_i = \langle \hat a_i, \vec v\rangle $$

 

$$ \text{proj}_{\hat a_i} ~\vec v = \frac{\langle \vec v, \hat a_i\rangle^*}{\|\hat a_i\|^2}\hat a_i=\langle \hat a_i, \vec v\rangle \hat a_i=\alpha_i\hat a_i $$

Operadores Lineares

Linearidade:

$f\{\alpha \vec v+\beta \vec w\}=\alpha f\{\vec v\}+\beta f\{\vec w\}$

 

$$\Rightarrow f\{\vec v\}=f\{\sum_{i=1}^n\alpha_i\hat a_i\}=\sum_{i=1}^n\alpha_if\{\hat a_i\}$$

Revisão de Álgebra Linear

Operadores Lineares

Se

$$f_i=f\{\hat{a}_i\}, \forall i=1,\ldots,n$$ então $$f\{\vec v\}=\alpha_1f_1+\alpha_2f_2+\cdots+\alpha_nf_n$$

Revisão de Álgebra Linear

Operadores Lineares

Representação matricial:

$$\vec w = f\{\vec v\}$$
$$\Rightarrow\sum_{m=1}^n w_m\hat e_m= \sum_{p=1}^n v_pf\{\hat e_p\}$$
$$\langle \hat e_k , \sum_{m=1}^n w_m\hat e_m\rangle = \langle \hat e_k,\sum_{p=1}^n v_pf\{\hat e_p\}\rangle$$
$$\Rightarrow w_k =\langle \hat e_k, \sum_{p=1}^n v_pf\{\hat e_p\}\rangle $$
$$\Rightarrow w_k =\sum_{p=1}^nv_p\langle \hat e_k, f\{\hat e_p\}\rangle $$

Revisão de Álgebra Linear

Autovalores e Autovetores

Se $\vec v$ é autovetor de $f\{\cdot\}$ então:

$$f\{\vec v\}=\lambda\vec v$$ ou $$Fv=\lambda v$$

em que $\lambda$ é o autovalor associado a $\vec v$.

Base de Autovetores

Seja $A=\{\vec a_1, \vec a_2,\cdots,\vec a_n\}$ uma base ortogonal formada pelos autovetores do operador linear $f\{\cdot\}$. Então,

$$ \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} w_1\\ w_2\\ \vdots \\ w_n \end{bmatrix}}_{w}=\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} \langle \vec a_1,f\{\vec{a}_1\}\rangle & \cdots & \langle \vec a_1,f\{\vec{a}_n\}\rangle \\ \langle \vec a_2,f\{\vec{a}_1\}\rangle & \cdots & \langle \vec a_2,f\{\vec{a}_n\}\rangle \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \langle \vec a_n,f\{\vec{a}_1\}\rangle & \cdots & \langle \vec a_n,f\{\vec{a}_n\}\rangle \end{bmatrix}}_{F}\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} v_1\\ v_2\\ \vdots \\ v_n \end{bmatrix}}_{v} $$
$$ \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} w_1\\ w_2\\ \vdots \\ w_n \end{bmatrix}}_{w}=\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} \langle \vec a_1,\lambda_1\vec{a}_1\rangle & \cdots & \langle \vec a_1,\lambda_n\vec{a}_n\rangle \\ \langle \vec a_2,\lambda_1\vec{a}_1\rangle & \cdots & \langle \vec a_2,\lambda_n\vec{a}_n\rangle \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ \langle \vec a_n,\lambda_1\vec{a}_1\rangle & \cdots & \langle \vec a_n,\lambda_n\vec{a}_n\rangle \end{bmatrix}}_{F}\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} v_1\\ v_2\\ \vdots \\ v_n \end{bmatrix}}_{v} $$
$$ \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} w_1\\ w_2\\ \vdots \\ w_n \end{bmatrix}}_{w}=\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} \lambda_1\langle \vec a_1,\vec{a}_1\rangle & \cdots & \lambda_n\langle \vec a_1,\vec{a}_n\rangle \\ \lambda_1\langle \vec a_2,\vec{a}_1\rangle & \cdots & \lambda_n\langle \vec a_2,\vec{a}_n\rangle \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ \lambda_1\langle \vec a_n,\vec{a}_1\rangle & \cdots & \lambda_n\langle \vec a_n,\vec{a}_n\rangle \end{bmatrix}}_{F}\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} v_1\\ v_2\\ \vdots \\ v_n \end{bmatrix}}_{v} $$
$$ \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} w_1\\ w_2\\ \vdots \\ w_n \end{bmatrix}}_{w}=\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} \lambda_1 & 0 &\cdots & 0 \\ 0 & \lambda_2 &\cdots & 0 \\ \vdots &\vdots & \ddots & \vdots\\ 0 & 0& \cdots & \lambda_n \end{bmatrix}}_{F}\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} v_1\\ v_2\\ \vdots \\ v_n \end{bmatrix}}_{v} $$

Operação Inversa

$$\vec v=f^{-1}\{\vec w\}$$

Operação Inversa

$$ \begin{split} \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} v_1\\ v_2\\ \vdots \\ v_n \end{bmatrix}}_{v}=\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{\lambda_1} & 0 & \cdots & 0\\ 0 & \frac{1}{\lambda_2} & \cdots & 0\\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & \cdots & \frac{1}{\lambda_n} \end{bmatrix}}_{F^{-1}} \underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} w_1\\ w_2\\ \vdots \\ w_n \end{bmatrix}}_{w} \end{split} $$ ou, olhando para cada coordenada, $$ v_i=\frac{1}{\lambda_i}w_i,\quad \forall i=1,\ldots, n $$
